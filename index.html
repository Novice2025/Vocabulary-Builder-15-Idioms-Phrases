#1 GET AWAY FROM IT ALL

MeaningExample
To go on vacation or take a break from work and duties.I have been so busy these days. I really just want to get away from it all.
To escape daily routine.He decided to get away from it all and went camping.

#2 KICK BACK AND RELAX

MeaningExample
To relax completely and enjoy oneself.What do you want to do after work? I just want to kick back and relax.
To lean back and relax comfortably.On Sundays my dad normally just kicks back and relaxes.

#3 HIT THE TOWN

MeaningExample
To go out and have fun, usually in a busy city/town.We decided to hit the town last Saturday night for dancing.
To go out for shopping, dancing, or dining.It's my birthday! Let’s hit the town.

#4 OFF THE BEATEN TRACK

MeaningExample
In a place where few people go; far from roads and towns.The guest house we stayed at was completely off the beaten track.
A remote or unusual location.We found a great vacation spot off the beaten track.

#5 SOAK UP SOME SUN

MeaningExample
To go outside and sit in the sun to get a suntan.This summer I want to go to the beach and soak up some sun.
To relax in the sun's warmth.He decided to soak up some sun this afternoon.

#6 HAVE A LOT ON ONE’S PLATE

MeaningExample
To have a lot of work or many problems to deal with.I have a lot on my plate these days, so I can't take on new projects.
To be very busy with multiple responsibilities.He has a lot on his plate, so he is not able to meet anyone.

#7 BRING HOME THE BACON

MeaningExample
To earn a salary or provide financial support for a family.If he stays at home with the kids, someone else will have to bring home the bacon.
To earn money at a job.I’ve got to get to work if I’m going to bring home the bacon.

#8 EXPERIENCE IS THE BEST TEACHER

MeaningExample
Life experiences teach more effectively than books or school.Mistakes may happen, but always remember that experience is the best teacher.
Practical knowledge is superior to theoretical knowledge.The older I get, the more I realize that experience is the best teacher.

#9 LEARN SOMETHING BY HEART

MeaningExample
To have something memorized perfectly.The piano player had to learn the piece of music by heart so he could perform it.
To know something word-for-word.Do you know all the words to the song? Yes, I learnt it by heart.

#10 THE SHOW MUST GO ON

MeaningExample
An event or activity must continue despite problems or difficulties.Even if it rains, the show must go on.
Resilience and commitment to completion.No matter what happens, we must ensure the show must go on.

#11 A SHOW STOPPER

MeaningExample
A performance or part of a performance that is extremely good.Her speech in class was a real show stopper.
Something impressive that commands attention.His song was a show stopper.

#12 CREAM OF THE CROP

MeaningExample
The best of the best; the highest quality available.This car is the cream of the crop.
The most excellent part of a group.These three students are the cream of the crop in their class.

#13 CLINGY

MeaningExample
To hold onto someone or something very tightly (emotionally or physically).He said that she was too clingy for him.
Sticking close to a person or surface.She wore a really clingy top.

#14 TO BE IN THE LIMELIGHT

MeaningExample
To receive attention and interest from the public; at the center of attention.He’s been in the limelight recently, following the release of his new movie.
To be the main focus of public scrutiny.Sally loves being in the limelight.

#15 BUTTER SOMEONE UP

MeaningExample
To flatter someone excessively in hopes of receiving a special favor.A student tried to butter the teacher up before asking for an extension.
To be overly nice to win favor.She buttered up her boss again to get a better assignment.
